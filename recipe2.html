Kenyan Chapati

Delicious layered flatbread perfect for any meal

Prep: 30 mins
Cook: 20 mins
Makes: 8-10
Difficulty: Easy

Chapati is a beloved staple in Kenyan cuisine, enjoyed with virtually any dish from stews to curries. This unleavened flatbread is slightly layered and has a delightful chewy texture. My mother taught me how to make chapati when I was young, and it remains one of my favorite comfort foods to prepare and share.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp sugar (optional)
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil, plus extra for cooking
  • 1 1/2 cups warm water (approximately)
  • Extra flour for dusting

Instructions

  1. Make the Dough

    In a large bowl, combine the flour, salt, and sugar (if using). Make a well in the center and add the oil. Mix the oil into the flour using your fingertips until it resembles breadcrumbs.

  2. Add Water

    Gradually add warm water while kneading until the dough comes together. Continue kneading for about 5-7 minutes until the dough is smooth and elastic.

  3. Rest the Dough

    Cover the dough with a damp cloth or plastic wrap and let it rest for at least 30 minutes. This makes the dough easier to work with and results in softer chapatis.

  4. Divide the Dough

    After resting, divide the dough into 8-10 equal portions and roll each into a smooth ball.

  5. Roll and Oil

    On a floured surface, roll out one ball into a thin circle, about 8 inches in diameter. Spread about 1/2 teaspoon of oil over the surface of the circle.

  6. Create Layers (Optional)

    For layered chapati, fold the circle into quarters (like folding a paper into a triangle), or roll it up like a rope, then coil it into a spiral. Press down gently and let it rest for 5 minutes. Then roll it out again into a circle about 8 inches in diameter.

  7. Cook the Chapati

    Heat a flat pan or griddle over medium heat. Place the chapati on the hot pan and cook for about 30 seconds or until small bubbles form. Flip it over and cook for another 30 seconds.

  8. Add Oil and Finish Cooking

    Apply a little oil to the top surface, then flip again. Press the edges with a spatula or clean kitchen towel, rotating the chapati. The chapati should puff up. Add a little more oil if desired.

  9. Keep Warm

    Remove the chapati from the pan and place it in a container lined with a clean kitchen towel to keep warm. Cover with the towel while you make the remaining chapatis.

  10. Serve

    Serve warm with your favorite curry, stew, or dish of choice.

Tips & Variations

  • For softer chapatis, knead the dough thoroughly and let it rest properly.
  • You can add grated carrots or spinach to the dough for colorful, nutritious variations.
  • If your chapati doesn't puff up, the dough might be too wet or too dry. Adjust accordingly next time.
  • Leftover chapatis can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 days. Reheat on a pan or in the microwave before serving.
  • In Kenya, chapatis are often served with beef stew, chicken curry, or beans. They're also delicious with tea for breakfast!
