Kenyan Pilau

A fragrant spiced rice dish that's perfect for celebrations

Prep: 20 mins
Cook: 45 mins
Serves: 6
Difficulty: Medium

Pilau is a beloved dish in Kenya, often prepared for special occasions and celebrations. This aromatic rice dish is infused with a blend of spices and cooked with meat for a flavorful meal that's both satisfying and impressive. My grandmother taught me this recipe, and I've added a few modern touches while keeping the authentic flavors intact.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice, washed and soaked for 30 minutes
  • 500g beef or goat meat, cubed
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 3 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 tbsp pilau masala (or make your own with cumin, coriander, cinnamon, cardamom, and cloves)
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 2 potatoes, peeled and cubed (optional)
  • 4 cups beef or chicken stock
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Meat

    In a large pot, heat the oil and add the sliced onions. Cook until golden brown. Add the cubed meat and brown on all sides. Season with salt.

  2. Add Aromatics

    Add the minced garlic, grated ginger, and pilau masala. Stir well to coat the meat and cook until fragrant, about 2 minutes.

  3. Add Vegetables

    Add the diced tomatoes and cook until they soften. If using potatoes, add them now and stir to combine.

  4. Add Rice

    Drain the soaked rice and add it to the pot. Stir gently to coat the rice with the spice mixture.

  5. Add Stock

    Pour in the beef or chicken stock. The liquid should be about 1 inch above the rice. Adjust salt if needed.

  6. Cook Rice

    Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover the pot, and simmer for about 20-25 minutes or until the rice is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.

  7. Rest and Serve

    Remove from heat and let it rest, covered, for 10 minutes. Fluff the rice with a fork, garnish with fresh cilantro, and serve hot.

Tips & Variations

  • For vegetarian pilau, replace the meat with vegetables like carrots, peas, and bell peppers.
  • You can use chicken instead of beef for a lighter version.
  • Authentic Kenyan pilau has a brown color. If yours is too light, you may need to brown your onions more.
  • Serve with kachumbari (Kenyan salsa) or yogurt on the side for a complete meal.
