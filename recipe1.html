Kenyan Chapati with Beef Stew

A comforting combination of flaky flatbread and rich, hearty beef stew that's perfect for any meal.

Ingredients

For the Chapati:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 1/4 cup oil (plus extra for cooking)

For the Beef Stew:

  • 500g beef, cubed
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 potatoes, cubed
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups beef broth or water
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions

For the Chapati:

  1. In a large bowl, mix flour, salt, and sugar.
  2. Add oil and warm water gradually, kneading to form a soft, elastic dough.
  3. Cover and let rest for 30 minutes.
  4. Divide the dough into 8 equal portions and roll each into a ball.
  5. Roll out each ball into a thin circle (about 8 inches in diameter).
  6. Heat a pan over medium heat, and cook each chapati with a little oil until golden brown spots appear on both sides.
  7. Keep warm by wrapping in a clean cloth.

For the Beef Stew:

  1. Heat oil in a large pot and brown the beef cubes on all sides.
  2. Add onions, garlic, and ginger and saut√© until fragrant.
  3. Stir in curry powder and coriander, cooking for another minute.
  4. Add tomatoes and cook until they break down into a sauce.
  5. Add carrots, potatoes, bell pepper, salt, and pepper.
  6. Pour in beef broth or water, bring to a boil, then reduce heat.
  7. Simmer covered for 1-1.5 hours until meat is tender and vegetables are cooked.
  8. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

Nutritional Information

Per serving (1 chapati with a portion of stew):

  • Calories: 450 kcal
  • Protein: 25g
  • Carbohydrates: 48g
  • Fat: 18g
  • Fiber: 5g
  • Sodium: 480mg
